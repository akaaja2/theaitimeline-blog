March 2026 has been one of the busiest months in AI history. Here's a summary of the major developments.

## Major Model Releases

**OpenAI GPT-5.4 "Thinking"** — Released March 5. Features a 1-million-token context window and autonomous multi-step workflow execution. Scored 75% on OSWorld-V (human baseline: 72.4%). OpenAI also open-sourced two models: `gpt-oss-120b` (single-GPU capable) and a 20B variant that runs on high-end consumer laptops. ([source](https://renovateqr.com/blog/ai-model-releases-2026))

**Anthropic Claude Opus 4.6** — Scores 80.8% on SWE-Bench Verified, ahead of GPT-5.4's 77.2%. Launched with multi-agent parallelism in Claude Code, memory across conversations for all users, and integration into Microsoft PowerPoint and Excel. Anthropic is now approaching $19B in annualized revenue. ([source](https://llm-stats.com/llm-updates))

**Google Gemini 3.1** — Multi-tier release spanning Flash-Lite (2.5× faster, $0.25/M tokens) to Deep Think, which scored 90% on IMO-ProofBench Advanced for graduate-level math reasoning. Gemini 3.1 Pro offers 2× reasoning improvement over Gemini 3 Pro at $2/$12 per million tokens. ([source](https://llm-stats.com/llm-updates))

**DeepSeek V4** — Launched ~March 3. 1 trillion total parameters with only 32B active per token, fewer than V3 despite the larger scale. ([source](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-models-march-2026-releases))

**NVIDIA Nemotron 3 Super** — Announced at GTC on March 11. 120B-parameter hybrid MoE model with 12B active parameters. Scores 60.47% on SWE-Bench Verified and 91.75% on RULER at 1M-token context. Open weights under NVIDIA's Nemotron license. ([source](https://www.crescendo.ai/news/latest-ai-news-and-updates))

**LTX 2.3** — Open-source 22B-parameter video model from Lightricks. Generates 4K video at 50 FPS with synchronized audio in a single pass, 8 denoising steps on the distilled variant. Free for commercial use. ([source](https://www.crescendo.ai/news/latest-ai-news-and-updates))

## Product & Tool Updates

- **Google AlphaEvolve** — A Gemini-powered coding agent using evolutionary algorithms, quietly deployed inside Google's infrastructure. Has recovered 0.7% of Google's worldwide compute and sped up a key Gemini kernel by 23%. ([source](https://llm-stats.com/llm-updates))
- **Google Genie 3** — New world-simulation model producing several minutes of interactive 3D environments at 720p/24fps, up from 10–20 seconds in Genie 2. ([source](https://llm-stats.com/llm-updates))
- **Manus Desktop App** — Manus's AI agent now runs locally, interacting with files and applications directly on-device. ([source](https://www.crescendo.ai/news/latest-ai-news-and-updates))
- **Google Workspace CLI** — Launched March 7. Bundles 40+ agent skills for connecting AI to Google cloud services, positioned as a simpler alternative to MCP setups. ([source](https://www.crescendo.ai/news/latest-ai-news-and-updates))
- **Adobe Firefly Custom Models** — Public beta lets teams train image generators on their own brand assets. ([source](https://www.crescendo.ai/news/latest-ai-news-and-updates))
- **Microsoft MAI-Image-2** — New text-to-image model from Microsoft's superintelligence team with improved realism and text rendering. ([source](https://www.crescendo.ai/news/latest-ai-news-and-updates))

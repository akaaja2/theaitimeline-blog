## Mistral Releases Open-Source Text-to-Speech Model

French AI company Mistral released Voxtral TTS, its first open-weight text-to-speech model, on March 26. The 4-billion-parameter model supports nine languages — English, French, German, Spanish, Dutch, Portuguese, Italian, Hindi, and Arabic — and can clone a voice from as little as three seconds of reference audio.

Mistral is releasing the full model weights, allowing companies to run Voxtral TTS on their own servers without sending audio to a third party. In human comparison tests, the model scored higher on naturalness than ElevenLabs Flash v2.5 at similar latency (70ms for a 500-character sample). It is available via API at $0.016 per 1,000 characters and on Hugging Face as an open-weights download.

The release puts Mistral in direct competition with ElevenLabs, Deepgram, and OpenAI for enterprise voice-agent workloads.

## Salesforce Overhauled Slack with 30 AI Features

Salesforce announced a major AI-driven update to Slack on March 31, adding 30 new features to Slackbot in what the company called its most ambitious release since the 2021 acquisition. The updated Slackbot is powered by Anthropic's Claude and targets the same workplace productivity space as Microsoft Copilot.

The new capabilities extend a January update that gave Slackbot agentic abilities such as drafting emails and scheduling meetings. Slack's engineering approach centers on "context engineering" — selectively feeding relevant channel history, files, and messages into the model's context window to improve response relevance. Starting in April, the updated Slackbot is rolling out to users on Slack's free and Pro plans.

Salesforce CEO Marc Benioff presented the update personally, framing it as the company's answer to Microsoft's growing AI push into enterprise messaging.

## ChatGPT's Ad Pilot Surpasses $100M ARR

OpenAI's advertising pilot hit $100 million in annualized recurring revenue less than two months after launching in the U.S., according to a CNBC report from March 26. The company is now working with more than 600 advertisers and says it has seen no impact on user trust metrics related to privacy.

Ads appear at the bottom of ChatGPT responses, are clearly labeled, do not influence the model's answers, and are not shown to users under 18 or near sensitive topics such as health or politics.

To scale the business, OpenAI hired Dave Dugan — formerly Meta's VP of Global Clients and Agencies — as its VP of Global Ad Solutions. Dugan reports to COO Brad Lightcap. The ad revenue represents a new diversification effort for a company that has historically relied on subscriptions and API access.

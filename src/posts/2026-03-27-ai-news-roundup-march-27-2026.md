Today's biggest story is an accidental leak from Anthropic, but there's more: Apple is widening its AI partnerships, Oracle is embedding agents into its database, and the Model Context Protocol has crossed a major milestone.

## Anthropic's "Claude Mythos" Leak

Anthropic accidentally exposed details of an unreleased model called **Claude Mythos** after a draft blog post was left in a publicly-searchable data store. Anthropic attributed the leak to a misconfigured content management system.

Internal documents describe Mythos as "by far the most powerful AI model we've ever developed," with dramatically higher benchmark scores than any prior Claude model in coding, academic reasoning, and cybersecurity tasks. The model is tied to a new capability tier internally codenamed **Capybara** — positioned above the current Opus line.

The leak also included warnings that Mythos could meaningfully accelerate offensive cybersecurity threats by identifying and exploiting vulnerabilities at speed. Cybersecurity stocks fell sharply on the news. Anthropic has not confirmed a release date. Sources: [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/03/26/anthropic-says-testing-mythos-powerful-new-ai-model-after-data-leak-reveals-its-existence-step-change-in-capabilities/), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/27/anthropic-cybersecurity-stocks-ai-mythos.html), [The Decoder](https://the-decoder.com/anthropic-leak-reveals-new-model-claude-mythos-with-dramatically-higher-scores-on-tests-than-any-previous-model/)

## Apple Opens Siri to Rival AI Assistants

Apple plans to allow competing AI assistants — beyond its existing ChatGPT integration — to connect to Siri in iOS 27. The move would let users route requests to third-party models directly through the Siri interface. Source: [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-26/apple-plans-to-open-up-siri-to-rival-ai-assistants-beyond-chatgpt-in-ios-27)

## Oracle AI Database 26ai

Oracle launched **Oracle AI Database 26ai** with persistent memory for AI agents and a no-code **Private Agent Factory** for building internal agents without writing code. The release reflects a broader trend of database vendors embedding agentic infrastructure directly into storage layers. Source: [Radical Data Science](https://radicaldatascience.wordpress.com/2026/03/26/ai-news-briefs-bulletin-board-for-march-2026/)

## MCP Crosses 97 Million Installs

The Model Context Protocol has surpassed **97 million installs**, and every major AI provider now ships MCP-compatible tooling. What started as an experimental standard has become the de facto interface layer for connecting AI agents to external tools and data. Source: [Crypto Integrated](https://www.cryptointegrat.com/p/ai-news-march-26-2026)

## Anthropic Wins Injunction Against Trump Administration

A federal judge in San Francisco granted Anthropic a preliminary injunction on March 26, temporarily blocking the Trump administration's ban on federal agencies using Claude models.

The dispute stems from Defense Department contract talks that broke down after the DOD demanded unrestricted access to Claude across "all lawful purposes." Anthropic sought assurances that its models would not be used for fully autonomous weapons or domestic mass surveillance. The DOD responded by designating Anthropic a supply chain risk and banning agencies from using its tools.

Judge Rita Lin called the supply chain risk designation "likely both contrary to law and arbitrary and capricious," writing that the statute does not support "the Orwellian notion that an American company may be branded a potential adversary and saboteur of the U.S. for exposing a disagreement with the government." She ruled the ban constituted First Amendment retaliation. The order is paused for seven days to allow the government to appeal. Sources: [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/26/anthropic-wins-injunction-against-trump-administration-over-defense-department-saga/), [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-26/anthropic-wins-court-order-blocking-trump-ban-on-use-of-ai-tool), [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/03/26/nx-s1-5762971/judge-temporarily-blocks-anthropic-ban)

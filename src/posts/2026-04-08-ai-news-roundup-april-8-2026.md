The first full week of April brings new search competition, expanded capabilities in workplace AI, and rare cooperation among frontier AI companies to defend against model extraction.

## Yahoo Launches Scout, an AI-Powered Search Engine

Yahoo has re-entered the search market with Scout, an AI-powered answer engine built on Anthropic's Claude. Announced in early April, Scout is designed to deliver personalized responses with supporting links, emphasizing direct answers over conversational interaction.

The product targets a segment already crowded with similar engines — Perplexity, Google's answer engine features, and OpenAI's SearchGPT. Scout differentiates by integrating across Yahoo's ecosystem of email, news, and portal services. The rollout began April 3.

Source: [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-03/yahoo-search-ai-engine-scout-launch)

## Microsoft Upgrades Copilot with Multi-Model Collaboration

Microsoft announced that Copilot can now orchestrate multiple AI models — including OpenAI's GPT and Anthropic's Claude — within a single workflow. Two new features support this:

- **Critique**: One model generates a response while another reviews it for accuracy and consistency.
- **Model Council**: Side-by-side comparisons of responses from multiple models for the same prompt.

The company also announced three new MAI models: MAI-Transcribe-1 (speech-to-text across 25 languages), MAI-Voice-1, and MAI-Image-2. MAI-Transcribe-1 delivers state-of-the-art accuracy on automatic speech recognition. All three are available through Microsoft's Foundry platform.

Sources: [Microsoft AI Blog](https://microsoft.ai/news/today-were-announcing-3-new-world-class-mai-models-available-in-foundry/), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/04/microsoft-copilot-multi-model-ai/)

## Google Releases Gemma 4, an Advanced Open-Weights Model

Google released Gemma 4, its most advanced open-weights AI model family. Built on the same architecture as Gemini 3, Gemma 4 is optimized for complex reasoning tasks and designed to enable autonomous AI agents running locally on low-power devices.

Gemma 4 competes directly with Llama 3.5 and other open-source alternatives, emphasizing on-device deployability and reduced inference costs compared to closed-model competitors.

Source: [The Decoder](https://the-decoder.com/google-releases-gemma-4-open-weights-ai-model/)

## OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google Team Up Against Chinese Model Copying

In rare cooperation, OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google announced they are sharing information to detect and prevent Chinese companies from extracting results from frontier US AI models through a technique called adversarial distillation. The effort operates through the Frontier Model Forum, an industry nonprofit the three companies founded with Microsoft in 2023.

The coordinated defense reflects a shift in competitive dynamics: while the US AI companies remain rivals, they face a common threat from state-backed and commercial entities in China attempting to reverse-engineer their models without authorization.

Sources: [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-06/openai-anthropic-google-unite-to-combat-model-copying-in-china), [The Decoder](https://the-decoder.com/openai-anthropic-and-google-team-up-against-unauthorized-chinese-model-copying/)

## Anthropic Expands Compute Capacity with Google and Broadcom

Anthropic announced a major expansion of its compute infrastructure through a new agreement with Google and Broadcom. According to a Broadcom SEC filing, the deal includes 3.5 gigawatts of compute capacity, with new processing power coming online in 2027.

The expansion reflects soaring demand for Claude. Anthropic's annualized run-rate revenue reached $30 billion in early April — a jump from $9 billion at the end of 2025. Separately, Anthropic unveiled Project Glasswing, a coalition of technology and cybersecurity companies given early access to a cybersecurity-focused version of its Claude Mythos model.

Sources: [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/07/anthropic-compute-deal-google-broadcom-tpus/), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/06/broadcom-agrees-to-expanded-chip-deals-with-google-anthropic.html)

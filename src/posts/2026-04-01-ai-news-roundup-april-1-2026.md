The end of March and start of April bring a second security incident at Anthropic, a record-setting capital raise at OpenAI, and major funding rounds in legal AI and robotics.

## Anthropic Leaks Claude Code's Internal Source Code

One week after accidentally exposing details of its unreleased Claude Mythos model, Anthropic suffered a second security incident. A packaging error caused an internal release of Claude Code to include roughly 500,000 lines of source code across approximately 1,900 files — the code behind Claude Code's "agentic harness," the software layer that wraps the underlying AI model and governs how it uses external tools.

No customer data or credentials were exposed, and Anthropic attributed the incident to human error rather than an external breach. Still, the leak is significant: the harness contains guardrails and behavioral instructions that shape how Claude Code operates, and a competitor could potentially study the code to improve their own agentic tools. Claude Code's run-rate revenue had grown to over $2.5 billion as of February. Sources: [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-01/anthropic-accidentally-releases-source-code-for-claude-ai-agent), [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/03/31/anthropic-source-code-claude-code-data-leak-second-security-lapse-days-after-accidentally-revealing-mythos/), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/31/anthropic-leak-claude-code-internal-source.html)

## OpenAI Closes $122 Billion Round at $852 Billion Valuation

OpenAI completed its largest funding round to date on March 31, raising $122 billion at an $852 billion valuation. The headline investors are Amazon ($50 billion, with $35 billion contingent on an IPO or AGI milestone), Nvidia ($30 billion), and SoftBank ($30 billion). An additional $3 billion came from individual investors through bank channels; Andreessen Horowitz, D.E. Shaw, and Microsoft also participated.

The round had been announced at $110 billion in February and grew to $122 billion as additional investors joined. An IPO later in 2026 is widely expected. Sources: [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-31/openai-valued-at-852-billion-after-completing-122-billion-round), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/31/openai-not-yet-public-raises-3b-from-retail-investors-in-monster-122b-fund-raise/), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/31/openai-funding-round-ipo.html)

## Harvey Raises $200M at $11 Billion Valuation

Legal AI startup Harvey raised $200 million led by Singapore's GIC and Sequoia Capital, valuing the company at $11 billion. The round closed just months after Harvey raised at an $8 billion valuation in December 2025, reflecting sustained investor appetite for AI tools targeting high-value professional services. Harvey builds AI for legal workflows including contract analysis, due diligence, and litigation research. Source: [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/25/legal-ai-startup-harvey-raises-200-million-at-11-billion-valuation.html)

## Physical Intelligence in Talks for $1 Billion Raise

Physical Intelligence, a San Francisco robotics startup founded by former Google DeepMind researchers, is reportedly in talks to raise approximately $1 billion at a valuation above $11 billion. The round would nearly double the company's $5.6 billion valuation from its November 2025 funding. Founders Fund is expected to participate, with Lightspeed Venture Partners also in discussions. Physical Intelligence builds AI models designed to give robots general-purpose dexterity across a range of physical tasks, rather than training models for single-purpose applications. Sources: [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-27/ex-deepmind-staffers-robotics-startup-in-talks-for-11-billion-valuation), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/27/physical-intelligence-is-reportedly-in-talks-to-raise-1-billion-again/)
